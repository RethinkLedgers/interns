# rcs-templates.com — New Concept GTM — Intern 1-Week Plan

> The product is **rcs-templates.com** — a library of **500+ ready-to-use RCS (Rich Communication Services) message templates** for marketing teams, agencies, SaaS, hospitality, events, and nonprofits. It's in **early access** (CTA: "Ask for Early Access"), works with providers like Twilio, Vonage, Bandwidth, and Sureshot, and has pro features for CSV personalization and AI/LLM-powered replies. Repo/site context: **github.com/RethinkLedgers/rcs-templates-website** (rcs-templates.com).

**Focus:** validate and drive demand for the new rcs-templates concept — get the right marketers into **early access**.
**Who we're going after:** marketing teams, **agencies**, SaaS growth/lifecycle teams, and hospitality/events/nonprofit marketers who send (or want to send) rich mobile messages.
**Region:** US-first (strongest RCS adoption via Google Messages), then English-speaking markets.
**Goal:** Customer Discovery → Market Analysis → Customer Outreach (email + organized campaigns)
**Objectives:** drive **early-access signups**, **initial calls/demos**, and **sales growth**.

## Tools you'll use

- **rcs-templates.com** (and the website repo) — know the product, the template library, the use-case industries, and the early-access flow cold so you can pitch it.
- **Apollo.io** — find marketing/agency/lifecycle decision-makers and enrich emails.
- **Twenty CRM** (`rethink-labs.twenty.com`) — log every account, contact, signup, and call.
- **Zoho** — send sequenced outreach in small, personalized batches.
- **Obsidian wiki** — write up market-analysis notes.

> Ground rules: work from the **msg2ai email**, keep all testing on **Test/staging**, never put API keys or contact lists in a tracked Git file, and never email at scale from production systems.

## Day 1 — Product immersion + define the ICP

*Outcome: you can explain rcs-templates in 2 sentences and know exactly who to target.*

- Explore **rcs-templates.com** end-to-end: the template explorer (industry/goal/tone filters), the "What is RCS?" explainer, the use-case industries, the provider options, and the early-access CTA.
- Learn the **RCS vs. SMS** story (richer media, branded sender, buttons/carousels, read receipts) — this is the core hook for marketers.
- Draft the **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):** marketing agencies (esp. SMS/mobile-marketing & lifecycle agencies), SaaS growth/lifecycle teams, and hospitality/events/nonprofit marketers; decision-makers in Growth, Lifecycle/CRM, Marketing Ops, and Founders at agencies.
- Write a one-paragraph **value proposition**: 500+ proven RCS templates so marketing teams launch rich campaigns in minutes instead of building from scratch.

## Day 2 — Market analysis (RCS / messaging-marketing landscape)

*Outcome: a short market brief that informs targeting and the pitch.*

- Map the **RCS adoption context**: US momentum (Google Messages, Apple's RCS support), what providers (Twilio, Vonage, Bandwidth, Sureshot) enable, and where SMS-heavy teams are looking to upgrade.
- Segment the buyers: agencies (resell/run campaigns for clients) vs. in-house SaaS/lifecycle teams vs. industry marketers (hospitality, events, nonprofits).
- Note the buying context: template libraries lower the barrier to RCS; CSV personalization + AI replies are differentiators; early access creates urgency.
- Deliverable: **1–2 page market brief** in the Obsidian wiki (segments, top accounts, hooks, objections — e.g. "we already use SMS", "is RCS supported on iPhone?").

## Day 3 — Customer discovery (build the target list)

*Outcome: 60–80 qualified accounts and 120+ contacts in the CRM.*

- Use **Apollo** to pull mobile-marketing/SMS agencies, SaaS lifecycle teams, and industry marketers; filter by title (Head of Growth, Lifecycle/CRM Manager, Marketing Ops, Agency Founder/MD).
- Qualify each account against the ICP (do they already send SMS/marketing messages and would benefit from rich RCS templates?).
- Enrich and import into **Twenty CRM** — one Company record per org, Contacts linked, tagged by segment + provider (if known).
- For the **top 15 accounts**, find a specific hook (their industry, a campaign they run, their current SMS stack) for personalization.

## Day 4 — Outreach prep (sequences, templates, CRM hygiene)

*Outcome: a ready-to-send, early-access-led campaign.*

- Write a **3-touch email sequence** (initial: the RCS-templates hook + early-access invite → value follow-up: a relevant industry template example → break-up), each ≤120 words. CTA: *get early access / book a 15-min walkthrough*.
- Build **2–3 segment variants** (agencies vs. SaaS lifecycle vs. industry marketers).
- Prepare a **demo asset**: a short Loom touring the template explorer + one industry use case, plus the early-access link.
- Set up CRM stages (Prospect → Contacted → Replied → Early Access → Call Booked) and a daily follow-up view. Confirm deliverability basics (send from the msg2ai email, small batches, no spammy links).

## Day 5 — Launch campaigns, book calls, report

*Outcome: campaign live, calls on the calendar, week wrapped up.*

- Send **Touch 1** to the full qualified list in **small batches** (e.g. 20–30 personalized), prioritizing the top-15 hooks.
- Work replies in real time → **book calls/walkthroughs** and push warm leads into **early access**; log every signup and outcome in Twenty.
- Capture **product feedback** from early conversations (which templates/industries resonate) and route it to the team.
- Write the **Friday progress report** in [`../progress-reports/`](../progress-reports/): emails sent, open/reply rates, early-access signups, calls booked, what resonated, objections, and next-week plan (Touch 2/3, expand list, refine by best-performing segment).
