# Free Claude Skills for Events Managers — IMEX Frankfurt — Intern 1-Week GTM Plan

> The hook is a **free pack of Claude Skills built for events managers** (e.g. RFP drafting, agenda builder, attendee-FAQ bot, budget tracker, post-event report writer). Give the skills away to start relationships, then convert warm leads into **msg2ai** ([`products/ai_ambassador/ai_ambassador_product_guideline.md`](../../../products/ai_ambassador/ai_ambassador_product_guideline.md)) calls and signups.

**Target event:** **IMEX Frankfurt** — the leading global trade show for meetings, incentives & events (Messe Frankfurt).
**Who we're going after:** **event organisers** and **travel agencies** attending/exhibiting at IMEX (meeting & event planners, MICE agencies, DMCs, incentive-travel agencies, hosted buyers).
**Goal:** Customer Discovery → Market Analysis → Customer Outreach (email + organized campaigns)
**Objectives:** drive **free-skill installs**, **initial calls / IMEX meetings**, **signups**, and **sales growth**.

> Targets are starter estimates — adjust with your manager after Day 1.

## Tools you'll use

- **Claude Skills** — package and test the free events-manager skill pack so you can hand it over and demo it.
- **msg2ai Test env** (`test.msg2ai.xyz`) — know the product the skills funnel toward. **Test only — never Prod.**
- **IMEX exhibitor & attendee directory** — the primary source list (plus the hosted-buyer program and the show's LinkedIn presence).
- **Apollo.io** — match IMEX orgs to decision-makers and enrich emails.
- **Twenty CRM** (`rethink-labs.twenty.com`) — log every account, contact, install, and meeting.
- **Zoho** — send sequenced outreach in small, personalized batches.
- **Obsidian wiki** — write up market-analysis notes.

> Ground rules: work from the **msg2ai email**, keep all testing on **Test**, never put API keys or contact lists in a tracked Git file, and never email at scale from Prod.

## Day 1 — Package the free skills + define the ICP

*Outcome: a ready-to-share free skill pack and a crisp picture of who to target at IMEX.*

- Build/curate the **free Claude Skills for events managers** (RFP drafting, agenda builder, attendee-FAQ assistant, budget tracker, post-event report). Test each one and write a 1-line description.
- Write simple **install instructions** a non-technical event planner can follow, plus a one-line bridge to msg2ai ("loved these? here's the AI Ambassador that runs your attendee comms").
- Draft the **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):** event organisers (corporate event/meeting planners, MICE agencies, PCOs) and **travel agencies** (DMCs, incentive-travel/business-travel agencies) attending IMEX Frankfurt.
- Write a one-paragraph **value proposition**: free, practical AI skills that save event managers hours per event — no cost, no lock-in.

## Day 2 — Market analysis (IMEX Frankfurt)

*Outcome: a short market brief that informs targeting and the pitch.*

- Confirm IMEX **dates, halls, and the hosted-buyer program**; understand who attends (planners, agencies, buyers) vs. who exhibits (destinations, venues, hotels, tech).
- Segment the attendee/exhibitor base into **event organisers** vs. **travel agencies**, by region and size.
- Note the buying context: planners are time-poor and AI-curious; a free, immediately useful skill pack is a low-friction door-opener; IMEX is a dense, time-boxed window to book meetings.
- Deliverable: **1–2 page market brief** in the Obsidian wiki (segments, top accounts, hooks, objections).

## Day 3 — Customer discovery (build the target list)

*Outcome: 60–80 qualified IMEX accounts and 120+ contacts in the CRM.*

- Pull the **IMEX exhibitor/attendee directory**; filter to event organisers and travel agencies, then match decision-makers in **Apollo** (Founder/MD, Head of Events, Operations, Marketing).
- Qualify each account against the ICP (do they run/handle events where these skills clearly save time?).
- Enrich and import into **Twenty CRM** — one Company record per org, Contacts linked, tagged by segment (organiser vs. agency) + region.
- For the **top 15 accounts**, find a specific hook (an event they run, a destination they sell, IMEX attendance) for personalization.

## Day 4 — Outreach prep (sequences, templates, CRM hygiene)

*Outcome: a ready-to-send, free-skills-led campaign tied to IMEX.*

- Write a **3-touch email sequence** (initial: free skills + "are you at IMEX?" → value follow-up: a second skill + meeting ask → break-up), each ≤120 words. CTA: *grab the free skills / book 15 min at IMEX*.
- Build **2–3 segment variants** (event organisers vs. travel agencies vs. hosted buyers).
- Prepare the **demo assets**: the free skill pack link/files + a short Loom showing one skill in action and the msg2ai bridge.
- Set up CRM stages (Prospect → Contacted → Skill Installed → Meeting Booked → Signup) and a daily follow-up view. Confirm deliverability basics (send from the msg2ai email, small batches, no spammy links).

## Day 5 — Launch campaigns, book meetings, report

*Outcome: campaign live, IMEX meetings on the calendar, week wrapped up.*

- Send **Touch 1** to the full qualified list in **small batches** (e.g. 20–30 personalized), prioritizing the top-15 hooks and anyone confirmed at IMEX.
- Work replies in real time → **book calls / IMEX meetings**; log every install and outcome in Twenty.
- Offer warm leads a **free Test signup** for msg2ai after they've tried the skills.
- Write the **Friday progress report** in [`../progress-reports/`](../progress-reports/): emails sent, open/reply rates, skill installs, meetings booked, signups, what resonated, objections, and next-week plan (Touch 2/3, expand list, IMEX on-site follow-ups).
